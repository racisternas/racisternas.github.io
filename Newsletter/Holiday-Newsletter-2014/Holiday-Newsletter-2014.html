Happy Holidays!

 
 
 

Dear friends, I was so far behind on work and other things that I began to doubt that I would ever get a newsletter out this year. However, I figured that this is the only way to stay in touch with all of you and say, hey, I miss you! So, here it goes.

2014 was a challenging year, it saw the departure of dear family members and friends. It also brought other challenges that took a lot of energy to overcome. All in all, the balance was positive. I successfully finished an 18-month long project at work, Fiona aced her sophomore year at Carlsbad High and is now a junior and beginning to think about college. Anyways, on with the 2014 story...

2013 ended and 2014 began with unseasonably high temperatures that made for great beach weather but also marked the beginning of another year of drought for California.

 
 

Have a great 2015!

I hope your Holidays have been joyous and spent in the company of those you love. I wish you all the best for the next year.

In February, my cousin Karolyn (left) and her friend Mimi from Germany stopped for a visit on their tour of the South West U.S.A. Karo lives in Berlin and works for Siemens while she studies at the university. She is my second cousin twice removed so do not bother looking for a resemblance.

In April, Fiona turned 16 and she celebrated with her friends. If you happen to know about the board game “Cards Against Humanity”, you can probably guess what brought up the reaction in the picture below.

In May, my friend Ramya (left) moved into her new home and invited Fiona and me to a fabulous house warming party with lots of delicious Indian food.

Later in May, we had a major heat wave. With temperatures above 100F and the hot Santa Ana winds blowing from the desert, it did not take long for the fires to begin burning in Carlsbad, San Marcos, and other communities. A lot of people were evacuated, dozens of homes burned, and there was at least one fatality. Unfortunately, it is a periodic occurrence in Southern California.

In spite of the heat, by the end of the month, the usual array of flowers was blooming in the Botanical Garden.

In June, Fiona completed her best portrait so far. A bust of Mads Mikkelsen, the actor who plays Hannibal Lecter in the Hannibal TV show. Naturally, Fiona worked entirely from pictures she downloaded from the web.

Also in June, Fiona and I visited the John Paul Getty museum in Los Angeles and enjoyed its collections and gardens.

July brings Comic-Con to San Diego, the largest convention of every topic related to fantasy. As we have done in the past, Fiona and I attended the convention. It always amazes me the amount of work people devote to their costumes.

Clockwise from the top-left: the San Diego convention center, a mother-daughter duo dressed as a Tardis and a Dalek from Dr. Who. Lou Ferrigno the original Incredible Hulk from the T.V. show in the 80s, and a magnificent costume of Maleficent, from the Disney movie of the same name.

July is also prime beach weather so I began visiting the beach to take pictures of the sunsets and the sea as I do every year. However, little did I know what would happen this time.

In August, I was leaving the office one day and I noticed the beautiful colors in the sky. I imagined the sunset would be photo-worthy so I rushed to the beach and I ran down the path to the shore. I stopped to take the picture on the left, because I thought it was very cute and then I continued running downhill until...

I hopped over a couple of steps and my foot made a snapping sound as it hit the ground and that was it. I limped back to the car, drove myself to the Emergency Room and after a while, they gave me the picture on the right. Yup, that was a crack on the bone.

The rest was quite predictable: six weeks of crutches, and no load bearing on my right foot.

Have I mentioned that Fiona has a driver’s permit now? That and the disabled placard came in handy. On the plus side, the crutches really improve your upper body strength. Now I have fully recovered and I am back to running.

In December, we had the opportunity to work at the San Diego Food Bank once again with a group of UCLA alumni. This time we were sorting food and doing some quality control (checking expiration dates).

Also in December, the Carlsbad High Orchestra performed its Holiday concert. Fiona is back in orchestra this year with a new teacher whom she enjoys very much.

After that, Fiona flew to Chile to spend the Holidays with her Grandmother. In the picture, they are standing in front of an old cannon battery in the city of Coquimbo, Chile.

In October I had the pleasure of meeting my old professor from University of Chile, Patricio Poblete. He has been working in the U. since the seventies and remarkably still looks just about the same. We had dinner in Santa Mónica and reminisced about the olden days, when the Computer Science department was barely getting started.
