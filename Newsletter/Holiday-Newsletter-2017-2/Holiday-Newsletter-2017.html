Hobbiton, was built as the set for  the Lord of the Rings and the Hobbit trilogies and is now a tourist destination. The place is as beautiful as it was made to look in the movies.

Next stop, New Zealand’s answer to Yellowstone National Park: Waiotapu Geothermal Reserve

The Champaine Pool

Lake Ngakoro

There are many different pools, some of them near boiling point (above) and some geysers. The minerals give each pool its own distinct color (the pictures were not manipulated).

Next up, an underground adventure in the Waitomo Caves including rappelling 90 feet down into the cave, spelunking, ziplining, and floating on an inner tube in an underground river while watching a constellation of glow worms on the ceiling above the pitch black cavern. I am not making this up. It was probably one of the most beautiful and serene sights I have ever seen.

Oh, there was also a surprise at the end. If you want to leave the cave tunnels, you have to climb up two 12-foot high water falls while the water is rushing past you, without falling and breaking your neck!

The next stop was Auckland, and then on to the Bay of Islands and a subtropical paradise. Sure, there was the private resort with its own beach and a yacht anchored ashore, but there was also much more: kickass scuba diving, the sleepy village of Russell, beautiful sunsets, great food...

And across the bay from Russell, the Waitangi Treaty Grounds, the location of the British governor’s house where the Treaty of Waitangi signed between the British Crown and the Maori was signed. Left, the view from the Governor’s house. Below, the Maori War Canoes.

The Treaty Grounds also contain the Maori Carved Meeting House where there is a daily ceremony that includes chanting and dancing of traditional Maori songs. Visitors are welcome as long as they are preceded by their leader who is supposed to give an introductory speech after facing off the Maori warriors.

Guess who got to be chosen as the visitor’s leader. The upside is that we got front row sears for the show and a photo op with the Maori group.

The non-Maoris in the left hand side of the picture are my friends Alexander and Claudia who were sightseeing with me.

The sight of the rivers of ice from the air was impressive and we also landed at the top of the Franz Josef Glacier.

In November, I attained a long cherished dream: to hike The Wave in the Pariah Canyon Wilderness in northern Arizona. (who said there was no excitement left in 2017?). It was a magnificent landscape. I was fortunate to be joined in this trip by my friends Tony and Kim.

The Wave is one of the top destinations for hikers from all over the world so getting permits to visit can take years and many attempts at the Bureau of Land Management’s permit lottery. In my case, I must have played the permit lottery at least 25 times over 8 years before winning. Visiting The Wave was in my bucket list for many years before I got my permit but it is such a beautiful sight that it was worth the wait.

You probably know by now that I love visiting the Colorado Plateau. The Paria Wilderness was new to me which made this trip doubly exciting. Besides the wave, we got to hike in beautiful Wire Pass slot Canyon. It is more than 50 feet deep and less than four feet wide in several places.

The next day, it was off to the Vermillion Cliffs.
