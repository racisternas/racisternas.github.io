Finally, it was time to return to Carlsbad and say good bye to Chile until next year.

This was my first time visiting the Grand Canyon in winter. The snow added a beautiful color contrast to the landscape.

Next stop was Moab Utah which happens to be very close to two great National Parks, Arches and Canyonlands. Both parks were closed to traffic due to the government shutdown in January but it was still possible to hike in and explore. The picture on the left shows the area of the park called Wall Street due to the tall rock walls that border a narrow valley.



At the bottom, is a view from Canyonlands entrance looking towards the East. You can see the La Sal mountains in the distance.

The way back from Utah was fun too. I spent two nights in Las Vegas and saw theBlue Man Group perform.

For the first (and perhaps only) time a home disaster arrived with perfect timing. I had my house inspected for termites right after Christmas and luckily, tenting the house did not become an issue because it all could be scheduled to coincide with my road trip. At the end of vacation, I came home to a termite-free house.

Fiona, meanwhile, spent the winter and Spring quarters doing research at the experimental psychology lab at UCSD. She produced an undergraduate Honors Thesis on the use of UWB sensors to track psychology research subjects while studying their physical interactions. She presented her results in June and got an award.

She will graduate from UCSD in June 2020 with a B.S. in Cognitive Science. Her concetration is in UI/UX (User Interface/ User Experience).

In May, my good college friend Alejandro Yachán came to visit me. This was a greatly anticipated visit so we had time to sightsee and enjoy the time together. As is customary for visitors, we went to the San Diego zoo and the Safari Park.

And we also visited the Science Center in Los Angeles.

Also during June, I had fun taking night shots of the San Diego County fair and the colorful rides there.

In July, I climbed Mt. San Gorgonio. At 11,503 ft. is the highest peak in Southern California. the climb was strenuous but the views from the summit were amazing.

In June, I lucked out and captured one of the most beautiful sunset pictures while on a walk in Carlsbad. At the far right you can see the contours of a rainbow since that was the last rainy day of a long rainy season.

Just as the Summer heat got started, the air conditioner decided to give up the ghost. The timing was not coincidental because, as you might suspect, you only turn on the AC when the Summer heat forces you to do it. The poor thing may have quietly expired months earlier in solitude and neglect.

There was also time for barbecues, and visits to the beach.

The end of August, also marked the first trip to Mexico in 2019. During the Fall, I got to visit Mexico City, Guadalajara, San Miguel de Allende and Guanajuato. México is a wonderful country, full of colors, music, rich culture, wonderful crafts, and exquisite food. Each place I visited was rich in local traditions and history.
