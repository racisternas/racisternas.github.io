Happy Holidays!

 
 
 

Dear friends, 2016 was a challenging year. My employer moved its offices to a new building giving me a longer commute, a smaller cubicle, and insufficient parking. Then they decided to force new tools and technology on all engineers and to top that, they had a layoff shortly after the move. Suffice it to say, that made a lot of people nervous and we ended the year with a number of defections. Things seem calmer now around the office but as the saying goes: “Lord grant me patience over the things I cannot control”. On the positive side, I resolved some nagging health issues this year. Also, Fiona graduated from High School with flying colors and is now a freshman at UCSD having a wonderful time with all the new learning opportunities available to her. She has a boyfriend named John whom you will meet in the pictures below.

2016 brought some long awaited rain to So. Cal. We are still officially in a drought but if this winter is generous with water, we will be finally turning the corner. The year was still warmer than previous years, and the water in the ocean was great the entire Summer.

Tony and I also visited Monument Valley, at the Northern Edge of the Navajo Reservation and straddling the state line between Arizona and Utah. It is a gorgeous place, where a number of Navajo families still make a living from the land with no services. 

This is the view of Monument Valley from a vantage point a few miles north. This place is called mile 13 and it is next to the mile marker sign.

A few miles further North, we stopped at Mexican Hat. The place is named after the balancing rock pictured on the right.

Mexican Hat is also a place of an outstanding geological feature pictured below. Where the San Juan River flows, a huge portion of the mountain sheared off creating this incredible zig-zag pattern that extends for miles (one of my favorite places in the world).

A couple of miles west of Mexican Hat, the San Juan River slowly meanders forming the remarkable landscape known as Goosenecks.

Further north, we stayed in Moab Utah and visited Arches NP where you can hike to Delicate Arch (right, I am below the left stone pilar), and also see Balanced Rock and Turret Arch (below and right-bottom).

West of Arches NP is Canyonlands N.P., the place where the Green River and the Colorado slowly approach one another until they finally meet at the confluence. Canyonlands is enormous and the pictures below only scratch its surface.
