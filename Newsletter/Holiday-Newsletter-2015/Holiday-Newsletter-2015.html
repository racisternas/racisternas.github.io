2015 was another year of drought for Southern California. The hot Summer days were complemented with very warm ocean waters that have remained unseasonably warm throughout the entire year. The warm water may be also a sign of our salvation from the drought because a strong El Niño warming of the Pacific Ocean is usually followed by major rainfall along California. All that warm ocean water could only mean one thing: beach time! On the way back from Chile, I visited Cusco and Machu-Picchu in Perú. I had never been to either place and the visit was a revelation. Cusco was the capital of the inca empire and later became a major city for the Spaniards. Nowadays, the core of the city is kept in its colonial splendor. The city is is more than 400 years old, clean, and beautifully kept. Full of buildings and relics from the Inca and Colonial periods. On top of that, the local cuisine is definitely world class. Here are some pictures from Cusco and nearby towns. Notice that the “gay” flag on some buildings has the rainbow colors in the opposite order. That is because it is actually the flag of the native peoples of the Andes.

This is the famous twelve-sided stone. The Incan stone masons would masterfully cut the stones to fit the available space and leave such a tight fit you cannot push a blade between the stones.

This is the fortress of Sacsayhuaman in the outskirts of Cusco.

The fabulous terraces of Pisac in the Sacred Valley where the Incas landscaped the entire mountainside.

The ruins at Ollantaytambo at the other end of the Sacred Valley.

But the crown jewel of Perú is the lost city of Machu-Picchu which was not found until the 20th century and therefore was not destroyed by the Spaniards.

One false step on the Inca trails and you are gone.
