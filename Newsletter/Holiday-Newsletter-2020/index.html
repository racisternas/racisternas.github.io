Through the years, I have been writing an end-of-year newsletter to reach out to all of you and say: "I am thinking about you even though we may not talk often". I try to use photography to illustrate what has caught my eye in the preceding twelve months and to share those experiences with you.

2020 was different than other years.

Happy Holidays!

A big part of the year was spent indoors while human contact was reduced to cautious hellos from a distance. Food shopping became a complicated adventure where toilet paper and cleaning supplies became treasured relics. There were no movies, theater, or other live performances and no travel opportunities. At the same time there was plenty of room for introspection or the pursuit of books, music, and podcasts. A great time to find streaming sites for foreign films. Hiking, at a safe distance, was an option.

Quietness painted our solitude.

It would have been a great vacation of the mind except for the endless stream of terrible news. People getting sick, some of them seriously ill, some dying. People losing their livelihoods and growing desperate.

Meanwhile, we also witnessed innumerable acts of courage, large and small, carried out by first responders, ER nurses, and all types of essential workers williing to risk their health to keep our complex work in motion in spite of the pandemic and the lockdown.

There was all that and so much more including protests, a nauseating presidential election cycle, wild fires, floods, locusts... well, maybe not locusts but 2020 was a smorgasbord of unpleasantness.

My year started with a repeat performance from previous years.

My refrigerator leaked while I visited Chile and I came back from my trip to find my kitchen floor and walls damaged by another hoome flood. The fourth one in 8 years. Maybe some divine punishment?

Soon, I was caught in a frenzy of activities involving my home insurance, contractors, shopping for materials, scheduling painters, planning to vacate my home while the floor was torn up, looking for a place to stay. But not all was bad news at that point. I had previously planned a blitz of a vacation at the end of January (which unbeknownst to me, would be the last time I would leave town for a while).

The last week of January, I headed to San Diego airport and boarded a flight to Prague, a city I had always longed to visit. As most of you know, the place has been around for over a thousand years and the core of the city is full of baroque buildings, bridges and the enormous Prague castle. Wandering around the city is a lot of fun. The buildings in the historic core of Prague are beautiful and very well kept and the city is full of works of art in the streets.
